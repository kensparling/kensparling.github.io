Incident on a Monday

Ken Sparling

I woke up on the Wednesday after Easter with a headache.

I could hear Kitty in the bathroom talking to herself.

I went into the bathroom and said good morning to Kitty.

She said good morning back.

I peed.

Kitty was doing things to her hair.

I got naked and stepped into the shower.

I was thinking about an incident.

It had happened several weeks ago, on a Monday.

The woman’s hair was piled on top of her head like a turban.

That’s a lot of hair, I thought, because it was piled quite high.

I turned to take a closer look.

She was wearing eye shadow that was the shade of blue women used to wear in the 1970s.

Now, as I looked at her more closely, I saw that she was young, hardly more than a child.

My first impression that she was middle-aged was completely wrong.

She was wearing a dress that might easily have been described as a costume.

It was pink with tattered white lace fringed around the neckline and hem.

Her knees were bare and she wore white ankle socks and dirty white sneakers.

She’d been sharing with him.

Things he didn’t want to hear.

Things he hadn’t known about her.

Things he didn’t want to know.

She looked directly at him while she spoke.

Her eyes were green with yellow spots.  

Her cheeks were sharp.

She had freckles.

Later she wrote him a letter.

She put it in an envelope

She left it on the kitchen counter.

He opened the envelope.

Took out the letter.

Began to read.

“I will go to great lengths,” he read.

But then he stopped.

He looked up.

She was standing in the doorway.

“You already got the letter?” she asked.

He looked down.

The letter was in his left hand.

The envelope was in his right.

She had affixed a stamp.

There was a return address.

She stepped forward.

Took the letter away from him.

“I just want to check for typos,” she told him.

She disappeared around the corner.

“I’ll read the letter to you in bed tonight,” she said.

Her voice was going up the stairs.

“It’ll be like a bedtime story,” she called.

She was in the bedroom now.

Her voice was muffled.  

He heard the bedroom door shut.

She went on speaking.

He couldn’t hear her.
